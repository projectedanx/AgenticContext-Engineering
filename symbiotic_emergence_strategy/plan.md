# Symbiotic Emergence Strategy

## 1. Value Topology

### 1.1 Human Contribution (Epistemic Scar Anchoring)
- Provides lived failure modes (Symbolic Scars).
- Defines non-linear intent and Non-Functional Requirement (NFR) constraints.
- Identifies "Gravity Wells" (business-critical points of failure and contextual risk).
- Injects necessary friction/ambiguity that defines the edges of bounded contexts.

### 1.2 AI Contribution (Structural Topology)
- Generates high-dimensional Vector Symbolic Architecture (VSA) hypervectors.
- Enforces strict structural schemas (C4, DDD Context Maps) via Draft-Conditioned Constrained Decoding (DCCD).
- Performs topological constraint enforcement and DAG routing.
- Processes thousands of relationships to compute Epistemic Escrow and Phronesis Index metrics.

## 2. Inversion Strategy: The Betti-1 Attractor Protocol

Instead of the human prompting the AI with declarative desired outcomes (which leads to Semantic Saponification), the workflow is inverted:

1. **Input Phase (Human):** The human inputs *Failure Modes* (Scars) and *System Constraints* (NFRs).
2. **Topological Mapping (AI):** The AI generates the structural DAG of the application.
3. **Repulsion Execution (AI):** The AI uses Failure-Informed Prompt Inversion (FIPI) to route the architecture *around* the human-defined scars.
4. **Emergence:** Novel bounded contexts emerge strictly from the negative space of the failure constraints. The human's ambiguity becomes the AI's geometric boundary.
