# KIRA-7 Emergence Strategy: Human-AI Symbiosis

## What: The Concept Value of AI and Human
The Agentic Context Engineering Workbench relies on a fundamental thermodynamic epistemic boundary between Human and AI.
- **Human Value (High-Entropy Intent):** The human provides the chaotic, high-entropy intent, the operational friction, the business contradictions, and most importantly, the "Scars" (Symbolic Scar Registry). Only humans experience the real-world consequences of a missing X-Lark-Signature or an expired tenant_access_token at 3 AM. This is the domain of teleological goals (G) and operational context.
- **AI Value (Low-Entropy Topology):** The AI cannot invent the operational friction, but it excels as a "Structural Extruder". It enforces constraints (Anionic Architecture), validates schema (DCCDSchemaGuard), and guarantees deterministic execution without "Semantic Saponification". It weaves the high-entropy human intent into low-entropy, fault-tolerant state machines.

## Why: The Necessity of Friction
Neither can succeed alone. Without AI, the human gets bogged down in boilerplate, schema validation, and syntax errors, failing to maintain the strict invariants required by APIs like Feishu Open Platform. Without Human Scars, the AI generates generic, naive "vibe code" that fails in production (e.g., missing URL verification challenge or token caching). The combination creates a paraconsistent logic system where human failure modes actively repel the AI into structurally sound topologies.

## How: Inversion for Emergence
We invert the standard generative AI relationship. Instead of asking the AI to "write a Feishu bot," we supply the AI with a "Lattice of Refusal" (G⁻). We define exactly what it MUST NOT do (e.g., NEVER output Feishu card JSON without passing it through DCCDSchemaGuard).
We implement the KIRA-7 Sovereign Agent Preset, which codifies the Feishu Open Platform invariants into a persistent agent identity.
